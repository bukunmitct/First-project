Timeline

  • 1642 - Isaac Newton was born on a Christmass day, in Woolsthrope, Lincolnshire, England. He was very weak at birth and wasn't expected to live
  • 1653 - Newtons creativity begins in 1653. He attends King's school in Grantham
  • 1653 - While in Grantham, Newton stays with Mr Clark. He build wooden models, carves sundails, draws, and builds a waterclock. After seeing a windmill, he builds a working mode of his own
  • 1661 - Newton enrolls at Cambridge University, at the age of 16.
  • 1665 - He recieves a bachelor's of art degree.
  • 1666 - Newton returned to Woolsthorpe (1665-1666), Cambridge closed down because of the plague breakdown in London. He during that period experiments the origin of color, nature of gravity, and creates what we know as calculus today.
  • 1667 - Cambridge reopen in 1667, and Newton returns to study. He is given a fellowship, a special award where he was paid to study. In 1669 he was made professor of mathematics
  • 1671 - Newton joins the royal society of London. Newton builds an improved version of a reflecting telescope, his friend Dr barrow takes it to the royal society of london, the society were so impressed they invited Newton to join the society
  • 1687 - Newton published his major work, PRINCIPIA
  • 1689 - Newton runs for parliament and wins a seat. He serves for one year.
  • 1693 - Around 1693 his health begins to fail and his mind wander, some people think it was a breakdowwn caused by mecury poisoning from some of his experiment
  • 1696 - He was appointed warden of the mint.
  • 1703 - He became the president of the Royal society. Also published his writings with light in a book called OPTICKS.
  • 1705 - Newton was given the highest honour in the land. He became a Knight, the first british scientist to be knighted. He is now known as SIR ISAAC NEWTON
  • March 20,1727 - Newton died at the age 84. He was burried at the famous Westminster abbey. Sir Isaac Newton will be rememberd as a brilliant man whose work in math and physics would continue to influence the world
